Kim Ki-Duk (December 20, 1960 - December 11, 2020) was a South Korean film director, screenwriter, and producer. He was known for his idiosyncratic art-house films, which often explored themes of violence, sexuality, and spirituality. Kim's films have won numerous awards at film festivals around the world, including the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival for Pieta (2012).

Kim Ki-Duk started his career as a screenwriter and then went on to direct his first feature film, "Crocodile" in 1996. Since then, he has directed over 20 films, many of which have won critical acclaim and international recognition. Kim Ki-duk's films are known for their visceral and often controversial content, as well as their unique visual style and symbolism.
